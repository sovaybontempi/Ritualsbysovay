A Morning Intention: A Daily Prayer for the Pagan Witch

May 24, 2026 · Daily practice · Prayer · Ancestors · Guides

I have been looking for a morning practice that actually fits my life — something that names the relationships I am actually in. The guides, the ancestors, the crawling things on my altar. Something that sets intention without pretending I have everything figured out.

This is what I came up with. It takes about five minutes to say aloud. It draws on four of the Thich Nhat Hanh mindfulness trainings — reverence for life, mindful consumption, true speech, and care for the body. It is a conversation I am having with myself and with the presences I work with, every morning before the day begins.

I offer it here because others might find it useful as a template — something to adapt into their own relationships, their own guides, their own intentions. The structure matters more than the specific words. Name who you are in relationship with. State what you are choosing. Mean it.

Who this is for

Anyone with a daily practice that has fallen fallow. Anyone who wants something spoken rather than silent. Anyone who works with ancestors, guides, or the natural world and wants a morning moment of acknowledgment before the noise of the day takes over.

You do not need to share my specific guides or my specific path. The prayer can be rewritten entirely. What I am offering is the shape of it.

"Name who you are in relationship with. State what you are choosing. Mean it."

The prayer

Spoken aloud. Approximately five minutes. Best done before the phone, before the news, before anything that asks something of you.

✦   ✦   ✦

I wake into this day.

I am held. I am not alone in this. The spirits and teachers that walk with me are already here, already present in this room and in this body, and I want to name them before I name anything else.

Guanyin — mother of compassion, she who hears the cries of the world — I honor you. I ask that your compassion move through me today, as something I actually practice. Let me meet what is hard with openness. Let me meet what is ugly with something softer than judgment.

To she who has walked the path — thank you for walking ahead of me. I receive what you carry.

To my brother ancestor — thank you for pulling me forward on the days I am tired. Today I will let you.

To Helko and Eleko, the nonbinary gods of technology — I ask for your help today with the devices and the platforms and the scrolling and the reaching. Help me use these tools with intention. Help me put them down when I am done.

To the trees — my oldest teachers — I am listening.

To the beetles, the wasps, the crabs, the snails, the ants, all things that crawl and carry and build — I see you on my altar. I see you in the world. I am your student.

✦   ✦   ✦

Today I choose reverence for life. I will not harm carelessly — not with my hands, not with my words, not behind someone's back in a conversation that costs them something they will never know was taken. If I speak of someone today, let it be with fairness. If I find someone difficult, let me look for the suffering underneath the difficulty. Compassion does not require that I like everyone. It requires that I try.

Today I choose to consume with care. What I take in — food, conversation, media, distraction — shapes the quality of my attention. I will try to notice when I am feeding something that diminishes me, and I will try to choose differently.

Today I choose true speech. I will not exaggerate. I will not gossip to make myself feel closer to people. Connection built on someone else's diminishment is not real connection.

Today I choose care for this body. This is the most important practice I have right now. I am learning — slowly, imperfectly — that rest is not laziness, that nourishment is not indulgence, that a body in pain cannot do the work it came here to do. I commit today to one thing that is genuinely restorative, rather than just efficient.

✦   ✦   ✦

Life is precious. I say it because I believe it and I want to remember that I believe it. The practice matters. The art matters. The aerial work, the spell work, the altar, the craft — these are the shape of a life I have chosen. I will do at least one thing today that is part of that life.

I am here. I am awake. Let the day begin.

✦   ✦   ✦

Notes from practice

The section naming the guides is the part that requires the most personal adaptation. Guanyin, she who has walked the path, my brother ancestor, Helko and Eleko, the trees, the crawling things — these are my relationships. Yours will be different. The point is to say the names. To make the acknowledgment real by speaking it aloud.

The four intentions drawn from the mindfulness trainings are choices I am renewing each morning. A choice implies I am in an ongoing relationship with my own values, and that tomorrow morning I get to choose again.

Five minutes is enough. It does not need to be longer to be real.

Note: This prayer is written from my path and my relationships specifically. Take what serves you, leave what does not, and rewrite it entirely if that is what your practice needs. A morning intention that does not sound like you will not hold.
