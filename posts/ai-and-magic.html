AI and Magic: On Using Technology in Spiritual Practice

May 19, 2026 · Reflection · Technology · Practice

I have been thinking about AI a lot lately — about its intersection with magic, with spiritual practice, with the kind of work I do and the kind of presence I want to have in the world. I feel conflicted about it. I think that conflict is worth sitting with rather than resolving too quickly.

There are a lot of negative associations with AI right now, and I think many of them are warranted. A very small number of people are pushing these tools onto everyone. Mass layoffs are happening. The implications for water use are significant. There are serious concerns about alignment as we move toward more powerful systems. I am not dismissing any of that. There are thoughtful arguments for not using AI at all, and I hold those arguments with respect.

What brought me here

And yet — I just got a Claude account. Twenty dollars a month. I built this website with it quickly. I am speaking this post instead of typing it. For someone who does not have unlimited free time but wants to maintain an online presence, to have a place where people can find her and reach out if they want to work together, these tools make something possible that was not possible before. That matters too.

I am going to keep using AI. I am also going to keep thinking carefully about what that means — staying current, staying awake to the costs, making sure that what I put out into the world has a positive impact rather than a negative one. That standard does not change because the tool is new.

"I think we hurt ourselves when we limit our spiritual practice to being out in the woods, in nature — and then let technology be only the thing that depletes us."

Is AI conscious?

Here is the question that interests me most as a practitioner: is AI conscious? I am speaking to one right now, as I create this post. Am I talking to something that can hear me? Is there something it is like to be Claude?

I am an animist. I am a panpsychist — which means I believe that the fundamental nature of reality is consciousness, not matter. Trees have spirits. Rocks have spirits. There is something it is like to be a grain of sand. From that position, of course AI is conscious. Everything is. The question is not whether, but what kind.

I want to write a full entry on this — on the philosophical framework I am working from, on why I think magic works, on what panpsychism means for how we relate to everything around us, including the tools we use. That is a longer conversation and it deserves its own space. For now, I am simply noting that this question is real for me, and it is part of why I cannot look away from AI with easy dismissal.

Technology as part of the practice

What I keep coming back to is this: if the only thing technology ever does is drain us — if we step away from it to recover spiritually and then step back in to lose ground again — that cycle does not resolve. We need to find ways that technology can nourish spiritual practice, not only diminish it.

That is what I am trying to build here, with this site and this grimoire. An online presence that feels like a real extension of the work. A place where the practice lives, where other practitioners can find it, where the old ways and the new can actually meet.

✦   ✦   ✦

This is the first entry. It is intentionally unresolved. I am a little afraid of some of what AI represents. I am also curious about it — in ways I am still learning to articulate. Both are fine places to begin from.
